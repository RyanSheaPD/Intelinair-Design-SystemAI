McLean County Hybrid Management Guide

Season-long management recommendations for the top 9 corn hybrids, grounded in aerial imagery and field-level yield analytics.
01
Agronomy · Planting

Planting & Population

All 9 hybrids in this lineup carry a >32K planting population recommendation per the program guide. Targets flex by soil productivity tier (NCCPI):

Soil TierTarget PopulationRationale
High NCCPI ≥ 0.80 34,000 – 36,000 High-productivity ground supports higher populations; flex/semi-flex ears adjust kernel count.
Medium NCCPI 0.60–0.79 32,000 – 34,000 Standard recommendation; balanced ear fill.
Low NCCPI < 0.60 30,000 – 32,000 Lower populations reduce plant-to-plant competition on stressed ground.
Field tip: Hybrid E and Hybrid F are semi-flex ear types that compensate at lower populations with longer ears and better tip fill — strong fits for variable-stand situations on tougher ground.
Note: Hybrid B and Hybrid C — confirm population guidance with your program advisor, as different hybrid families may have different optimal ranges than this lineup.

Nitrogen Management

Trial data strongly supports managing nitrogen as a system, not a single application. Three strategies, eight+ years of field trials:

Strategy Trials Yield Adv. ROI / Ac
N as a system
inhibitors + split + supplemental products
 263 trials · 9 yr +8.8 bu/ac Consistent
2-Pass vs 1-Pass 124 trials +6.8 bu/ac $14.74
Product C
Eastern IL only
 21 trials +2.2 bu/ac Supplemental
Poorly drained fields (E, I, Q) are highest risk for nitrogen loss via denitrification. A 2-pass system is especially valuable here — trial data show the response is more notable in wet years when increased denitrification and leaching occur.
Product C (labeled rate, in-furrow or foliar V2–V4) can supplement a traditional program, but the Eastern IL response averages just +2.2 bu — treat it as insurance, not a primary N source.
