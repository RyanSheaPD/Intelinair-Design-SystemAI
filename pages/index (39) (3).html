Field Tiers
Fields are classified by soil productivity and drainage into four tiers. Soil productivity sets the base tier. Poor drainage (poorly drained or worse) drops the tier by one level.
A Premium — soil productivity > 0.82, adequate drainage B Standard — soil productivity 0.72–0.82, or A downgraded by poor drainage C Challenged — soil productivity 0.62–0.72, or B downgraded by poor drainage D Marginal — soil productivity < 0.62, or C downgraded by poor drainage
Seed Efficiency
Efficiency = yield per 1,000 seeds planted. The sweet spot is 7.0–7.5 for corn and 0.45–0.55 for soybeans. Below means under-populated (room to increase). Above means over-seeded (can reduce without yield loss).
Hybrid Placement
Placement matches hybrids to fields based on two factors: (1) yield performance in the field’s tier vs the tier average, and (2) population fit — whether the hybrid’s optimal seeding rate aligns with what the field’s drainage classes need. The goal is to get every zone to 7.5 efficiency.
